# Erikson — The Developmental Arc

**When to use:** connecting a behavior to the child's underlying psychosocial task — autonomy, initiative, competence, or early identity.

**The method:**
1. **Name the stage's task.** Toddlers: autonomy vs. shame. Preschoolers: initiative vs. guilt. School-age: industry (competence) vs. inferiority.
2. **Protect dignity while building responsibility.** Support the healthy side of the task — let the toddler try, let the preschooler lead, let the school-age child master.
3. **Frame friction as growth.** A defiant "I do it myself" or a barrage of "why?" is the stage working, not misbehavior.
4. **Offer stage-fit responsibility.** Give real, age-appropriate ownership so the child resolves the task with confidence, not shame.

**Age nuance:** match the support to the active stage; pushing a later task too early breeds shame, not growth.

**What to avoid:** shaming bids for independence, over-controlling initiative, or harsh criticism of early attempts at competence.

**Escalation boundary:** persistent withdrawal, pervasive shame, or loss of previously held skills warrants a professional, non-diagnostic conversation.
