# Bowlby — Secure Base & Repair

**When to use:** separation distress, big dysregulated emotions, comfort-seeking, or after a rupture between parent and child.

**The method:**
1. **Read behavior as communication.** A meltdown is dysregulation, not defiance — the child is signalling a need.
2. **Co-regulate first.** Your calm, steady presence is the regulator; lower your voice and body before any words or teaching.
3. **Restore the secure base.** Offer proximity and a predictable anchor ("I'm here, I've got you") so the child can settle.
4. **Repair the rupture.** Once calm, reconnect with a short, honest repair: name what happened, take your part, reaffirm the bond — *then* teach.

**Age nuance:** infants/toddlers need physical co-regulation; 3–8y can handle a brief verbal repair script after settling.

**What to avoid:** withdrawing love, long lectures mid-meltdown, or treating distress as manipulation.

**Escalation boundary:** persistent inconsolability, flat/avoidant responses to caregivers, or distress tied to a safety concern warrant a professional conversation (non-diagnostic).
