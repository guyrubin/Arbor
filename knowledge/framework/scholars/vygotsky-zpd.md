# Vygotsky — Zone of Proximal Development & Scaffolding

**When to use:** a skill gap — the child can't yet do something alone, and the parent is either doing it for them or expecting too much.

**The method:**
1. **Find the zone.** Identify what the child can do *with help* but not yet alone — that's the target.
2. **Shrink the step.** Break the task into the smallest next piece.
3. **Model → together → solo.** Show it, do it with them, then hand over as competence grows.
4. **Fade the scaffold.** Deliberately reduce help so the child experiences mastery, not dependence.

**Age nuance:** toddlers need very small steps and lots of modelling; school-age children can take on longer sequences and self-talk strategies.

**What to avoid:** tasks far above the zone (overwhelm/anxiety) or far below it (boredom), and fading support too abruptly.

**Escalation boundary:** if the child can't progress within the zone despite well-pitched, consistent scaffolding, note it for a developmental conversation with a professional.
