# Bronfenbrenner — The Child's Ecosystem

**When to use:** the concern is clearly shaped by context — school, family stress, co-parent misalignment, multiple languages, illness, a house move, or institutional pressures.

**The method:**
1. **Map the systems.** Look at the child's microsystems (home, school, peers) and the links between them, not just the child.
2. **Find the real lever.** Often the most effective change is environmental — routine, schedule, sensory load, consistency across homes — not a demand on the child.
3. **Align the settings.** Reduce friction *between* settings: shared expectations between parents and between home and school.
4. **Buffer stressors.** Name the macro-stressors (move, transition, financial/illness strain) and protect the child's predictability through them.

**Age nuance:** younger children are most affected by the home microsystem; school-age children by peers and school.

**What to avoid:** locating the whole problem inside the child when the environment is driving it.

**Escalation boundary:** when ecosystem stressors are severe (instability, conflict, unsafe settings), coordinate with school and professional support — see the handoff tools.
