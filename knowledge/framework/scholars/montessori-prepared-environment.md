# Montessori — The Prepared Environment & Practical Autonomy

**When to use:** the child is capable of more independence than the current setup allows — power struggles around dressing, tidying, meals, or routines that the parent is doing *for* the child.

**The method:**
1. **Prepare the environment, not the child.** Make the right thing physically possible: low hooks, a stool at the sink, clothes in an open two-choice bin, snacks on a reachable shelf.
2. **Show, slowly, once.** Demonstrate the sequence without words, then let the child try without correction.
3. **Offer structured freedom.** Two acceptable choices ("the red shirt or the blue?"), not open-ended or no choice.
4. **Protect concentration.** When the child is absorbed in a task, don't interrupt to praise or help.

**Age nuance:** toddlers need very short sequences and lots of repetition; 3–6y can own a whole micro-routine (morning dressing, setting their plate).

**What to avoid:** doing it faster yourself, hovering, or correcting mid-task — these teach the child that they are not capable.

**Escalation boundary:** if a child cannot manage age-typical self-care despite a well-prepared environment and consistent practice, note it for a developmental conversation with a professional.
