# Piaget — Match the Demand to the Cognitive Stage

**When to use:** a parent is frustrated that a child "won't listen to reason," lies, or can't take another's perspective — often a stage mismatch, not defiance.

**The method:**
1. **Name the stage.** Roughly 2–7y is *preoperational*: concrete, egocentric, sometimes magical thinking; limited grasp of another's viewpoint, time, or abstract cause-and-effect.
2. **Teach through experience, not lectures.** Show and let them do; use objects, pictures, and short concrete language over explanations.
3. **Expect egocentrism.** A young child taking the bigger piece or struggling to share is developmentally typical, not selfish.
4. **Use stage-true consequences.** Immediate, concrete, and related to the action; long delayed or abstract consequences don't land yet.

**Age nuance:** around 7+ (concrete-operational) children handle logic, reversibility, and rules far better — expectations can rise accordingly.

**What to avoid:** moral lectures, abstract bargaining, or treating stage-typical limits as character flaws.

**Escalation boundary:** persistent, marked gaps from age-typical reasoning warrant a developmental screening conversation with a professional — framed as an observation, never a diagnosis.
