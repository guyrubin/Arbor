# Winnicott — The Good-Enough Parent

**When to use:** the parent is ashamed, exhausted, or chasing perfection; or the child leans hard on a comfort object.

**The method:**
1. **'Good enough' beats perfect.** Reliable, ordinary, repairing care is exactly what a child needs — not flawless care.
2. **Provide a holding environment.** Steady, non-anxious boundaries and predictability let the child feel safely contained.
3. **Honor transitional objects.** A pebble, plush, or blanket is healthy bridging support during separations — respect it, don't shame it.
4. **Tolerate manageable frustration.** Small, survivable frustrations (with you nearby) build resilience; rescue from real distress, not from every struggle.

**Age nuance:** transitional objects peak in toddlerhood and early childhood and naturally fade — no need to rush their removal.

**What to avoid:** self-criticism, over-functioning, or removing a comfort object abruptly to "toughen up" the child.

**Escalation boundary:** if parental guilt tips into persistent low mood, hopelessness, or burnout, surface caregiver support — the parent's wellbeing is part of the child's environment.
