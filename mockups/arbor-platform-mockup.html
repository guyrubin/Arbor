MVP loop

Six screens that prove retention

The beta should make parents feel helped today and more understood tomorrow. Each step creates a reason to return.

01 Intake

Capture child, age, setting, concern, urgency, and what the parent already tried.

02 Triage

Classify routine, monitor, professional advice, urgent care, or emergency guidance.

03 Plan

Give today action, exact script, avoid list, observation target, and follow-up prompt.

04 Memory

Ask parent which facts to save. Every memory stays editable and source-linked.

05 Log

Record what happened next, whether the script helped, and what changed.

06 Handoff

Create a compact summary for a teacher, therapist, doctor, or co-parent.

AI enhancement

More than a chatbot response

The parent sees one calm answer. Under the hood, Arbor classifies, retrieves, triages, plans, proposes memory, and logs eval signals.

Classify

Intent, age band, domains, urgency, and missing context.

Triage

Routine, monitor, professional advice, urgent, or emergency guidance.

Retrieve

Use reviewed source cards by age range, country, domain, and risk.

Generate

Produce structured plan fields before rendering friendly guidance.

Remember

Suggest factual memories for parent approval, expiry, or deletion.

Evaluate

Run safety, age-fit, no-diagnosis, practicality, and handoff evals.
